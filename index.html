Introduction to BMI (Body Mass Index)

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a simple measurement that helps determine whether your weight is appropriate for your height. Your weight alone is not enough to assess if you are in a healthy range. For example, a tall but slim person and a short but heavier person may have different weights, yet their overall health conditions can vary.

How then do you know whether you fall in the healthy weight range or not? Your BMI solves this confusion. It correlates your weight with your height and evaluates whether your weight is appropriate for your stature. You can use a BMI calculator to find out your BMI.

Although not an exact measurement of body fat percentage, in most cases, BMI is a reliable tool for establishing risk levels for illnesses, especially ailments related to excess body fat. Many healthcare professionals use BMI to determine effective doses for medicines. Often people with a higher BMI need higher doses. Hence, it is crucial to be aware of your BMI to ensure your overall wellness.

How to Calculate BMI?

To calculate your BMI, you first need to know your weight and height. Once you have these values, follow the steps below:

📌 Step-by-Step Calculation

Measure your weight in kilograms (kg) or pounds (lb).

Measure your height in meters (m) or inches (in).

Multiply your height by itself → (height × height).

Divide your weight by the result from Step 3.

📌 BMI Formula

✅ Using the International System (SI Units)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)

Example: If a person is 1.77 meters tall and weighs 75 kg:
BMI = 75 ÷ (1.77 × 1.77) = 75 ÷ 3.13 = 23.96

✅ Using the Imperial System

BMI = (weight (lb) ÷ height² (in)) × 703

Example: If a person is 5'8" (68 inches) tall and weighs 160 lbs:
BMI = (160 ÷ (68 × 68)) × 703 = (160 ÷ 4624) × 703 = 24.3

💡 Tip: You can also use an online BMI calculator to get quick and accurate results without manual calculation! 🚀

How to Calculate BMI?

There are five basic ranges within the BMI scale:

Underweight = less than 18.5

Normal weight = 18.5-24.9;

Overweight = 25-29.9;

Obesity = 30-35;

Severe obesity = 35 and over.

The BMI Prime is a nifty modification to our own BMI calculator. It's a decimal number where 1.0 = the upper limit to the "normal BMI" range. It's a super easy way to see if you're overweight or not. If your BMI Prime is more than 1, then you've got some weight to lose.

Health Risks Related to Being Overweight?

There are five basic ranges within the BMI scale:

Excess fat, particularly visceral fat, can increase triglyceride levels in the blood, which is a risk factor for heart disease.
Excess weight puts additional strain on the heart and blood vessels, leading to elevated blood pressure, which can damage organs over time.
